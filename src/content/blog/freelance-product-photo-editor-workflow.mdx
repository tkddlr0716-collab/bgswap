## The Freelancer's Bottleneck

Your current workflow probably looks like this:

1. Client sends 50-200 product photos
2. You open each one in Photoshop
3. Select the product with Quick Selection / Pen Tool / Select Subject
4. Refine edges
5. Remove background
6. Add new background (white, or whatever the client wants)
7. Add drop shadow
8. Export
9. Repeat 50-200 times

At 5-10 minutes per image, a 100-image batch takes 8-16 hours. You charge $2-5 per image, earning $200-500 for two days of repetitive work.

**The bottleneck isn't skill — it's the manual repetition.**

## When to Use AI vs. Manual Editing

Be honest with clients about what AI handles well and what still needs human skill:

### AI Handles Well (Automate These)
- Background removal for standard products (boxes, bags, bottles, electronics)
- White background generation (Amazon-compliant)
- Consistent background replacement across large batches
- Drop shadow generation
- Batch processing for catalog-style images

### Still Needs Human Editing
- Transparent products (glass, acrylic) — may need edge cleanup
- Complex compositions (multiple products arranged together)
- Color correction and white balance adjustment
- Retouching (removing scratches, dust, reflections)
- Creative compositing (lifestyle scenes, custom environments)
- Ghost mannequin cleanup for clothing

**The smart approach:** Use AI for the 80% that's repetitive background work. Spend your manual time on the 20% that requires creativity and skill.

## Quality Control Checklist

Even with AI, review every batch before delivering to clients:

- [ ] Clean edges around products (no halos or artifacts)
- [ ] Consistent shadows across all images
- [ ] No missing product parts
- [ ] Correct background color (matches client spec)
- [ ] Resolution matches client requirements
- [ ] File naming convention followed
- [ ] Spot-check 10-20% of images at full zoom

This review takes 10-15 minutes for a 100-image batch. It's the step that separates "I used a free online tool" from "I delivered professional work."
