Etsy is different from Amazon. On Amazon, pure white backgrounds are mandatory. On Etsy, they can actually hurt your sales.

Etsy buyers expect **warmth, personality, and craftsmanship.** Your photos need to reflect that — while still looking professional.

---

## The #1 Etsy Photo Rule

**Your first photo is your thumbnail.** It's the only thing shoppers see in search results. Everything — click-through rate, search ranking, sales — starts with that one image.

Make it:
- **Clear** — product is immediately identifiable
- **Bright** — well-lit, not dark or muddy
- **Clean** — minimal distractions
- **Consistent** — same style across all listings

## Backgrounds That Work on Etsy

### What Top Sellers Use

| Background | Best For | Vibe |
|-----------|---------|------|
| **Light gray** | Jewelry, ceramics, skincare | Clean but not clinical |
| **Warm cream/beige** | Textiles, candles, handmade goods | Cozy, handcrafted feel |
| **Wood texture** | Kitchen items, crafts, rustic products | Natural, artisan |
| **Marble** | Jewelry, cosmetics, premium items | Luxury, elevated |

- **Pure white** — feels "Amazon-ish," lacks the handmade warmth Etsy buyers expect
- **Busy patterns** — distracts from the product
- **Dark backgrounds** for small items — product gets lost

## Lighting for Etsy

Natural light wins on Etsy. It produces the warm, authentic look that matches the platform's vibe.

### The Window Setup

1. Place product near a window (indirect light)
2. **Reflector opposite the window** — white foam board bounces light back, fills shadows
3. Shoot during **golden hour** or overcast days
4. **Never use flash** — it kills the natural look

### For Jewelry and Small Items

- **Light tent/box** ($20-30) for even, shadow-free lighting
- Or DIY: white paper curved inside a cardboard box, lamp on top

## Using All 20 Photo Slots

Etsy gives you 20 photos per listing. Top sellers use all of them:

| Slot | Photo Type | Purpose |
|------|-----------|---------|
| 1 | **Hero shot** | Clean background, product clear | 
| 2-3 | **Lifestyle/styled** | Product in use, in context |
| 4-5 | **Detail close-ups** | Texture, stitching, quality |
| 6 | **Size reference** | Next to coin, hand, ruler |
| 7-8 | **Different angles** | Back, side, top |
| 9 | **Packaging** | What the customer receives |
| 10 | **Infographic** | Features, dimensions, materials |
| 11-15 | **Color variations** | Each color option gets its own photo |
| 16-20 | **Customer photos/social proof** | With permission |

### The Trick

Slots 1-5 get the most views. Put your best photos there.

## Styling Tips by Category

### Jewelry

- Flat lay on marble or linen background
- Include a mirror or metallic surface for reflection
- Show on a model (hand, neck, ear) for scale
- Close-up of clasp and hallmark

### Clothing & Textiles

- Flat lay OR on a hanger against clean background
- Show fold/drape to demonstrate fabric quality
- Include measurement photos
- Iron/steam before shooting (wrinkles kill sales)

### Ceramics & Home Goods

- Lifestyle: on a shelf, on a table with a plant
- White/gray background for the hero shot
- Show the bottom (stamp, maker's mark)
- Include a hand holding it for scale

### Candles & Skincare

- Warm, moody lighting works well
- Props: dried flowers, fabric, wooden surfaces
- Show texture (pour, texture, ingredients)
- Include the label readable in at least one shot

## Phone Photography for Etsy

You don't need a DSLR. Phone cameras in 2026 produce Etsy-quality photos easily.

**Key settings:**
- Portrait mode for hero shots (soft background blur)
- 2x zoom for detail close-ups (avoids distortion)
- Tap to focus on the product, not the background
- Edit in Lightroom Mobile (free) — bump brightness +10, contrast +5, warmth +3

Your photos affect search ranking:

- **File name matters** — rename from IMG_4532.jpg to `handmade-ceramic-mug-blue.jpg`
- **Alt text** — Etsy uses alt text for accessibility and search. Include keywords.
- **First photo determines thumbnail** — test which photo gets more clicks using Etsy's stats

## Common Mistakes

1. **Dark, moody photos** — Etsy search results are small thumbnails. Dark photos disappear.
2. **Too much styling** — Props should complement, not overwhelm the product.
3. **Inconsistent style** — Your shop page is a grid. If every listing looks different, it looks amateur.
4. **Only 3-5 photos** — You're leaving 15 slots empty. More photos = more information = more confidence = more sales.
5. **No size reference** — #1 reason for returns on Etsy is "smaller/bigger than expected."
