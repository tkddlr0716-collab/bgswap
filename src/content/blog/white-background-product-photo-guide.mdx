## Method 1: DIY White Background

### What You Need

- White poster board or foam board ($3-5)
- Window with natural light
- Phone camera
- Optional: clip-on ring light ($15-20)

### Step-by-Step

1. **Set up the sweep** — Place the board against a wall, curving it at the bottom. This eliminates the horizon line.
2. **Position near a window** — Indirect natural light from the side works best. Avoid direct sunlight (harsh shadows).
3. **Place product on the curved section** — Not too close to the background.
4. **Shoot slightly above** — 30-45 degree angle for most products.
5. **Check histogram** — The background should be near 255 (pure white) on the RGB scale.

### Limitations

- Rarely achieves true #FFFFFF — usually 240-250 range
- Shadows make the background gray near the product base
- Inconsistent between shoots (lighting changes)
- **For Amazon:** You'll still need software cleanup to hit #FFFFFF

## Method 2: Software Cleanup

After DIY shooting, clean up the white in Photoshop or a free tool:

### Photoshop Levels Adjustment

1. Open image
2. Image → Adjustments → Levels
3. Drag the white point (right slider) left until background hits 255
4. Be careful not to blow out the product

### Free Alternative: GIMP

Same process, free software. Colors → Levels → adjust white point.

### Limitation

Manual process. Works for 5-10 photos. Not scalable for 50+.

## Common White Background Mistakes

### 1. Off-White Background

Your "white" is actually RGB 240, 240, 240. Looks white to the eye, but Amazon's automated checker flags it.

**Fix:** Use a tool that guarantees #FFFFFF output, or adjust levels in post.

### 2. Gray Shadow Under Product

Natural shadows make the base area gray, even with a white setup.

**Fix:** Either remove shadows entirely or use controlled drop shadows that don't affect the background color.

### 3. Color Cast from Lighting

Warm (yellow) lighting makes white backgrounds creamy. Cool (blue) lighting makes them bluish.

**Fix:** Set white balance to "daylight" or "auto" before shooting. Correct in post if needed.

### 4. Product Too Small in Frame

Amazon requires the product to fill 85%+ of the image area.

**Fix:** Crop tightly or use a tool that auto-centers and sizes the product.

### 5. Wrinkled Background

Fabric backgrounds wrinkle. Paper backgrounds crease.

**Fix:** Use foam board (rigid) or AI background replacement (no physical background needed).
