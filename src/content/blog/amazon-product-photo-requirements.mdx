Getting your Amazon listing suppressed because of a bad product photo is one of the most frustrating experiences for sellers. You lose visibility, sales drop, and fixing it takes days.

This guide covers everything you need to know about Amazon's product photo requirements in 2026 — and how to make sure your images pass every time.

## Main Image Requirements

Amazon's **main image** (the first photo buyers see) has the strictest rules:

- **Pure white background** — RGB 255, 255, 255. Not off-white, not light gray. Pure white.
- **Product fills 85%+ of the frame** — No tiny products floating in white space.
- **No text, logos, or watermarks** — The product must speak for itself.
- **No props, accessories, or lifestyle elements** — Just the product.
- **Professional quality** — No blurry, pixelated, or poorly lit photos.
- **Minimum 1000px on the longest side** — 2000px+ recommended for zoom functionality.
- **JPEG, PNG, or TIFF format** — JPEG is most common.
- **sRGB color profile** — For consistent color display.

### Why Pure White Matters

Amazon's automated systems scan your main image. If the background isn't close enough to #FFFFFF, your listing can be:

1. **Suppressed** — Hidden from search results
2. **Blocked** — Prevented from going live
3. **Flagged for review** — Manual review queue (takes days)

The threshold isn't publicly documented, but sellers report that anything below RGB 250, 250, 250 can trigger issues.

## Secondary Image Guidelines

Secondary images (slots 2-9) are more flexible:

- Lifestyle/in-use shots allowed
- Infographics with text and callouts
- Size comparison images
- Different angles
- Package contents / what's included

**Pro tip:** Use secondary images to show your product on different backgrounds. A dark background for premium feel, warm tones for lifestyle — this is where background variety helps conversion.

---

## Common Mistakes That Get Listings Suppressed

1. **Off-white background** — Your "white" is actually light gray
2. **Shadow on white** — Heavy shadows make the background not pure white
3. **Cropped edges** — Product cut off at the frame edge
4. **Reflective surfaces** — Product sitting on a reflective table
5. **Background objects visible** — A wall corner, table edge, or wire
6. **Watermark from editing tool** — Free tools often add watermarks
