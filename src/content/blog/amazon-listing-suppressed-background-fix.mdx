## Why Amazon Suppresses Listings

Amazon's automated image scanner checks your main image against strict requirements:

| Check | Requirement | Common Failure |
|-------|------------|---------------|
| Background color | Pure white (#FFFFFF) | Off-white, gray, cream |
| Background objects | None allowed | Table edge, wall corner, wire |
| Product fill | 85%+ of frame | Product too small, too much whitespace |
| Text/logos | None on main image | Watermark from editing tool |
| Borders | None | Added frame or border |

The background check is the most common reason for suppression. Amazon's scanner is stricter than your eyes — what looks white to you might be RGB 245, 245, 245, which fails.

## Step 1: Check Your Background Color (2 Minutes)

Before re-uploading, verify your background is actually pure white.

### On Windows

1. Open image in Paint
2. Use the color picker (eyedropper) tool
3. Click on the background area
4. Check the RGB values — all three must be **255, 255, 255**

### On Mac

1. Open image in Preview
2. Tools → Show Inspector → click magnifier tab
3. Hover over background
4. Check RGB values

### If It's Not 255, 255, 255

Your background is the problem. Even 250, 250, 250 can trigger suppression.

## Step 2: Fix the Background (Choose One)

### Option A: Quick Fix in Photoshop (15 minutes)

1. Open image
2. Select → Color Range → click background
3. Adjust Fuzziness until all background is selected
4. Edit → Fill → White (255, 255, 255)
5. Deselect, check edges, export

**Problem:** Manual, time-consuming. Need Photoshop subscription.
